A BRIEF HISTORY OF BOOK PUBLISHING IN YABA COLLEGE OF TECHNOLOGY

How YABATECH Became a Pioneer in Printing and Publishing Education

If you walk through the vibrant corridors of Yaba College of Technology (YABATECH), you’ll notice a deep connection between tradition and innovation. From the walls of its historic printing studios to the buzzing digital design labs, this institution has played a groundbreaking role in book publishing and printing education in Nigeria. But how did it all begin? Let’s take a journey through time.

A Legacy of Printing Excellence (1952 - 1970s)
Long before digital publishing and e-books, YABATECH was already setting the foundation for Nigeria’s book publishing industry. The journey began in 1952 with the establishment of the Department of Printing Technology—one of the ?rst of its kind in West Africa.

Originally designed to train professionals for the booming print industry, the department focused on traditional printing techniques like letterpress, lithography, and bookbinding. Back then, students learned how to manually set type, ink presses, and produce high-quality printed materials—skills that were essential in an era dominated by newspapers, magazines, and physical books.

The Digital Revolution & The Future of Publishing (2000s - Present)
The 2000s brought the biggest shift in publishing since the invention of the printing press—digital technology. Traditional printing methods were being replaced by desktop publishing, digital printing, and online book distribution. YABATECH adapted swiftly, integrating Adobe In design, CorelDRAW, and digital publishing tools into its curriculum.
